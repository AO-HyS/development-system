# AOHYS Development System contract

Contract version: `0.5.0`

## Repository audit and explicit preparation

`audit-repository` is a read-only inspection of one product repository. It reports governing instructions and nested precedence, skills, agents, Factory droids, hooks, the six operational states, foreign-product residue, detected stack and commands, preview gaps, and readiness for Codex, T3Code, and Factory. File presence never proves catalog exposure, load, or behavioral influence; those states require fingerprint-matched operational evidence.

`initialize-repository` and `normalize-repository` are separate mutation operations with separate confirmations. They manage only `.development-system/repository.json`, `.codex/development-system/repository.md`, and `.factory/development-system/repository.md`. They preserve product domain, stack, package commands, release policy, and design files, and are deterministic and idempotent. They do not activate paid services.

React repositories receive React-specific quality guidance without replacing their visual system. Convex repositories receive validator, authorization, indexing, and bounded-read guidance. Codex is the native repository adapter, T3Code shares the Codex contract, and Factory receives a documented behavioral equivalent when a native capability is absent.

`improve-codebase-architecture` remains manual and proposal-only. It may inspect and propose deepening, but refactoring requires a later, separately authorized operation.

## Prior guarantees retained

The `0.4.0` observable harness adapters, operational validator, capability benchmark and roster, adversarial Implement Preview delivery loop, and private decision surfaces remain in force. The `0.3.0` lifecycle state machine and exact one-shot sensitive-operation grants also remain in force, together with canonical installation, skill provenance, six-state evidence, drift detection, reversible synchronization, and rollback.

Generated HOME and repository preparation state cannot redefine the canonical repository. Audit never repairs findings. Initialization or normalization does not authorize merge, release, production, destructive cleanup, or economic activation.
